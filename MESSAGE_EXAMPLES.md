## Understanding the Indicators

### Moving Averages (MA)
- **What it is:** The average price over a period of time
- **What it means:**
  - If short-term (10) > long-term (30): Prices are trending UP ‚Üí Good for buying
  - If short-term (10) < long-term (30): Prices are trending DOWN ‚Üí Good for selling

### RSI (Relative Strength Index)
- **What it is:** Measures if an asset is overbought or oversold (0-100)
- **What it means:**
  - RSI < 30: Very oversold ‚Üí Price may be too low, good to BUY
  - RSI > 70: Very overbought ‚Üí Price may be too high, good to SELL
  - RSI 30-40: Low but not extreme ‚Üí Could be good to buy
  - RSI 60-70: High but not extreme ‚Üí Could be good to sell
  - RSI 40-60: Neutral ‚Üí No strong signal

### Support and Resistance
- **Support:** The lowest price level (like a floor)
- **Resistance:** The highest price level (like a ceiling)
- **What it means:**
  - Price near support (bottom): Good time to BUY (price might bounce up)
  - Price near resistance (top): Good time to SELL (price might drop)

### Trend
- **Bullish:** Prices generally going up ‚Üí Positive momentum
- **Bearish:** Prices generally going down ‚Üí Negative momentum

## What to Do When You Get a Signal

### For BUY Signals:
1. Check if you actually want to buy this asset
2. Verify the current price matches what you see in the message
3. Consider your budget and financial goals
4. Don't invest more than you can afford to lose
5. Consider buying in smaller amounts (dollar-cost averaging)

### For SELL Signals:
1. Check if you actually own this asset
2. Consider your original investment goals
3. Think about whether you need the money now
4. Consider selling in parts, not all at once
5. Remember: taking profits is never a bad thing
